He walked alone — through rain, through silence, through days when even his own shadow felt too loud. Tired? So tired the earth pulled him down, and his eyes shut mid-step. But he didn’t stop. Not because he was stronger than others — just because he knew: if he sat down, no one would rise for him. And so — step by step, trembling but never falling — he arrived where the path had promised. It all worked out. Not easily. Not gracefully. But — it worked out. Because he kept walking. Even when he had no strength left. Especially when he had no strength left.
